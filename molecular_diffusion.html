Molecular Diffusion Simulation

The Molecular Diffusion simulation was written in JavaScript. It was created as a request from the school's science department, as a way to show how molecules of different sizes could diffuse through a permeable membrane over time, with several different possible factors, including temperature, size of molecules, whether active transport was enabled or not, and if the ports through the membrane were open. The simulation runs on a timer and pauses once the timer runs out.

I worked on the project with someone else, and we split the coding pretty evenly. I organized the code very similarly to how my 2D game dev projects were organized, with different classes for the molecule types and an update function for each one that ran every frame to update its position and the graphics. Outside of the coding itself, there was the organizational aspect of the project; we had to go back and forth with the teachers who wanted to use the simulation for their class, and take into account their feedback while developing it. Overall, we got extremely positive feedback when they used it in their classes.
